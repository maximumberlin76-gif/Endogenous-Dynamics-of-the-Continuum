In quantum mechanics, a multiplet represents a group of physically connected states possessing common fundamental characteristics and belonging to a single structure.

Different states of the multiplet are not independent objects.

Different states of the multiplet are interconnected manifestations of a single quantum structure.

Consequently, quantum multipletness represents not a set of separate objects, but a system of interconnected states of a single dynamic process.

In process logic, this principle has fundamental significance.

Observable forms of structural self-organization of Matter of Space Time do not arise from isolated essences.

Observable forms of structural self-organization of Matter of Space Time arise as the result of the coordinated interaction of a multitude of interconnected states of a single process.

It is precisely for this reason that quantum multipletness can be considered as the fundamental basis of the emergence of observable forms of structural self-organization of Matter of Space Time.

Consequently, quantum multipletness ensures not only the diversity of possible states.

Quantum multipletness ensures the possibility of the emergence of observable forms of structural self-organization of Matter of Space Time, the possibility of their dynamic retention and the possibility of their subsequent transformation into other observable forms of structural self-organization of Matter of Space Time.

In this logic, quantum multipletness acts as the first level of recursive structural self-organization.
